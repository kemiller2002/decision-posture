Adoption

Start without a rollout.

Decision Posture is designed to be adopted bottom‑up, without framework theater. Start with one decision and expand only when it pulls.

Principle

The goal is not to “implement a methodology.” The goal is to reduce irreversible risk under uncertainty.

Guardrail

If you can’t describe the decision, the constraints, and the trigger conditions, you don’t have alignment — you have a story.

Social safety

No one is asked to “agree.” People are asked to declare what would change their mind.

The bottom‑up path

  1. Pick one live decision. Choose something high-impact but not politically explosive. Keep it small enough to be reversible.
  2. Run a 30-minute posture check. Identify what’s admissible, what’s missing, what must be constrained, and what triggers reversal.
  3. Capture a single artifact. A one-page decision record beats 40 slides. The artifact is the memory.
  4. Do a short after-action. Not “what happened,” but “what we assumed” and “what would have changed the decision.”
  5. Repeat twice. Adoption begins when the same failure mode shows up again.

Signals you’re ready to expand

Pull

People ask for posture checks before high-stakes meetings.

Language

Teams reuse the vocabulary (constraints, triggers, admissibility) without prompting.

Artifacts

Decision records are referenced later, especially when narratives start to shift.

When not to do this

  • If leadership wants a “program” to signal change, rather than changing decisions.
  • If the organization punishes bad news — posture requires truth to be speakable.
  • If the decision is already politically decided and you’re being asked to create justification.

Want help picking the first decision?

A 30-minute call is usually enough to identify a low-drama decision with high signal.
