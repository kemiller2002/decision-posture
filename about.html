Why this exists

I built Decision Posture for the moments when certainty shows up too early — and the cost of being wrong is high.

In most organizations, decision quality isn’t limited by intelligence. It’s limited by pressure: deadlines, visibility, authority, fear of blame. Under pressure, teams drift into confident narratives, misuse data, and diffuse ownership — then act as if uncertainty disappeared.

Decision Posture is a way to make the decision environment safer without slowing teams down: name what’s admissible, constrain what data is allowed to do, keep uncertainty visible, and preserve accountability.
